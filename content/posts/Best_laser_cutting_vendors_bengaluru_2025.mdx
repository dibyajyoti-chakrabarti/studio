## Types of Laser Cutting Available in Bengaluru

Understanding which technology your part needs is the first step to finding the right vendor.

### Fiber Laser Cutting

Fiber laser is the dominant technology for metal cutting in Bengaluru in 2025. It handles:

| Material                    | Thickness Range | Typical Tolerance |
| --------------------------- | --------------- | ----------------- |
| Mild Steel (MS)             | 0.5mm – 25mm    | ±0.1mm            |
| Stainless Steel (SS304/316) | 0.5mm – 20mm    | ±0.1mm            |
| Aluminium                   | 0.5mm – 15mm    | ±0.15mm           |
| Copper                      | 0.5mm – 6mm     | ±0.15mm           |
| Brass                       | 0.5mm – 10mm    | ±0.1mm            |

**When to use fiber laser:** Any metal sheet cutting, structural profiles, enclosures, brackets, flanges, decorative metalwork. Fiber laser offers zero burr, minimal heat-affected zone, and tight tolerances on complex profiles.

### CO2 Laser Cutting

CO2 lasers handle non-metallic materials that fiber cannot cut:

- Acrylic (up to 25mm)
- Wood, MDF, plywood
- Rubber, foam, leather
- ABS, polycarbonate (some grades)
- Fabric and textiles

**When to use CO2:** Display stands, signage, packaging prototypes, acrylic enclosures, rubber gaskets, non-metallic components.

### Plasma Cutting

For structural steel above 20mm thickness where precision is less critical than speed and cost. Common in construction and heavy fabrication, less common for precision parts.

## What to Look for in a Laser Cutting Vendor in Bengaluru

Not all job shops are equal. Here's the evaluation framework MechHub uses when qualifying MechMasters in Bengaluru:

### 1. Machine Capability — Match Your Material and Thickness

The single most important specification is laser power. A 1.5kW fiber laser cannot cut 10mm MS cleanly. A 3kW machine may struggle above 15mm. Always confirm:

- **Laser power (kW):** 2kW (thin sheet, up to 6mm MS), 3–4kW (general purpose, up to 12mm), 6kW+ (heavy plate)
- **Bed size:** Common sizes are 1500×3000mm and 2000×4000mm. Check if your sheet size fits
- **Assist gas:** Nitrogen gives cleaner, burr-free cuts on SS and aluminium. Oxygen is faster on MS but can cause oxidation. Ask which they use for your material

### 2. DXF/STEP File Handling

A good vendor can work with your files directly. Red flags:

- Asks you to redraw in their software
- Can't handle STEP or DXF without conversion
- Requires you to specify nesting yourself

Green flags:

- In-house CAM programming team
- Can optimise nesting to reduce material waste (and pass the saving to you)
- Provides DFM feedback before cutting

### 3. Quality and Inspection

For precision parts, ask:

- Do they have a CMM (Coordinate Measuring Machine) or at minimum a height gauge and surface plate for first article inspection?
- Do they provide first article inspection reports on new jobs?
- What is their rework/rejection policy?

For prototype and low-volume work, a visual inspection against your drawing is typically sufficient. For production runs of 50+ pieces, ask for a documented quality procedure.

### 4. Lead Time Reliability

The most common complaint about Bengaluru laser cutting vendors is not price — it's delivery. "Ready in 2 days" often means 5–7 days. Ask:

- "What is your current capacity utilisation?" (A shop running at 90%+ will push your job)
- "Can you show me your last 5 jobs' delivery performance?"
- "What happens if my job misses the committed date?"

### 5. IP and Confidentiality

Your DXF or STEP file contains your product geometry. Many Bengaluru job shops do not have NDA practices — your design can be shared with a competitor, used to approach your client directly, or simply left unsecured on an open server.

For any product under development, always:

## Red Flags When Evaluating Laser Cutting Vendors in Bengaluru

### They Can't Quote Without Seeing a Drawing

A professional laser cutting vendor can give you a ballpark estimate based on material, thickness, sheet size, and complexity before you send a file. If they insist on a file for any indication at all, they may lack the internal expertise to do rough estimates — which suggests limited experience.

### No GST Invoice

Any vendor unwilling to provide a GST invoice is either unregistered (which limits their legal compliance) or deliberately avoiding documentation. As a business buyer, you lose your 18% input tax credit — effectively paying 18% more.

### Quoted Price With No Breakdown

A professional quote should itemise material cost, machine time, setup, and any post-processing. A single number with no line items is impossible to validate or negotiate.

### No First Article Process

For any new part, a first article (the first cut piece inspected against drawing before the full batch runs) is standard professional practice. A vendor who says "we just run the job" has no quality gate — your entire batch can be wrong before anyone checks.

### Extremely Low Prices With No Explanation

Prices 40%+ below market average are either a calculation error, a quality compromise, or a bait-and-switch. Always ask the vendor to explain their quote in detail. Legitimate cheap vendors can always explain why.

## How to Get the Best Laser Cutting Results in Bengaluru

### Prepare Your Files Correctly

**For DXF files:**

- All cut lines on a single layer, clearly separated from dimensions
- No open contours — every profile must be a closed loop
- No duplicate lines (causes double-cutting and burned edges)
- Minimum internal hole diameter: 1x material thickness (for 2mm SS, minimum hole = 2mm Ø)
- Minimum feature-to-edge distance: 2x material thickness

**For STEP files:**

- Model all sheet metal at true final thickness
- Do not include bend reliefs in STEP — provide a flat DXF and a bent 3D STEP separately

### Specify Clearly

Your purchase order or job card should include:

- Material grade (not just "stainless steel" — specify SS304 or SS316)
- Thickness in mm (confirmed, not nominal)
- Quantity
- Tolerance requirement (standard ±0.1mm is usually sufficient for laser)
- Edge finish required (deburring? polishing?)
- Assist gas preference (nitrogen vs oxygen for metal)
- Delivery requirement (ex-works or delivered to your address)

### Build a Relationship

The best laser cutting experience in Bengaluru comes from repeat business with a single vendor who knows your parts. After 3–4 jobs, a good MechMaster knows your file conventions, your quality expectations, and your delivery sensitivity — and will prioritise your jobs accordingly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the minimum order for laser cutting in Bengaluru?**
Most shops have a minimum job value of ₹500 – ₹1,500. For very small or simple parts, the setup cost dominates — ordering 10 pieces instead of 1 typically costs only 20–30% more in total while reducing per-part cost by 60%.

**What file format do Bengaluru laser cutting vendors accept?**
DXF is the universal format for 2D laser cutting. Always provide a DXF with all cut profiles on a single layer. STEP files are acceptable if you want the vendor to unfold a 3D model, but expect a ₹200–500 charge for unfolding services.

**How long does laser cutting take in Bengaluru?**
Standard lead time is 2–4 days for simple jobs up to 25 pieces. Complex profiles or large batches (100+) are typically 5–7 days. Same-day or next-day service is available for premium rates from some vendors.

**Is fiber laser or CO2 better for sheet metal in Bengaluru?**
Fiber laser is better for all metals — it is 3–4x faster than CO2 on steel and aluminium, produces a cleaner edge, and has lower operating cost. CO2 is only necessary for non-metallic materials (acrylic, wood, fabric) that fiber cannot cut.

**Can I get laser cutting and bending from the same vendor?**
Yes — many Bengaluru job shops offer combined laser cutting and CNC press brake bending services. Sourcing both from one vendor eliminates fixture-to-fixture variation and reduces lead time. Ask specifically for "laser + bending" capability when inquiring.

**What is the standard edge finish from fiber laser cutting?**
Standard fiber laser cutting on steel produces a clean, slightly oxidised edge. For a clean silver edge on stainless steel, request nitrogen-assist cutting. For a smooth, polished edge, request deburring or edge polishing as a post-process.
